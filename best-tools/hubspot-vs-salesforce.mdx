## Breadth vs conviction — the real split

The two brands win on different axes:

- **HubSpot Sales Hub = breadth.** ChatGPT names it in more of its CRM answers (37%). It shows up broadly across prompts and buyer conditions.
- **Agentforce Sales = conviction.** When ChatGPT does name it, it places it higher (average position 2.33). It appears less often but more prominently.

DECA Score combines both, so the ranking tips toward whichever axis is stronger that week. This week HubSpot's breadth advantage shrank (its score dropped −21.6) while Agentforce's conviction held — so Agentforce took #3. That's a snapshot, not a trend.

## What this means for your brand — in any category

- **Track breadth and conviction separately.** "Mentioned a lot" and "placed high when mentioned" are different wins that move independently — HubSpot leads one, Agentforce the other.
- **A volatile score is a warning sign.** HubSpot's ±16–22 weekly swings mean its position isn't locked; steadiness (Agentforce) compounds into rank.
- **Know the real ceiling.** In CRM, the top is held by Zoho and Dynamics; a #3/#4 race is a race for the "next name," not the lead.
- **One week's flip isn't a verdict.** The lead changed on a single week's drop — confirm direction across 2–3 weeks before acting.

Copy checklist (works in any category):

1. Measure both your mention rate and your average position — improve the weaker one deliberately.
2. Prioritize consistency: a steady mid-score outranks a volatile higher one over time.
3. Against entrenched leaders (here, Zoho and Dynamics), compete for the reachable slot — the stable #3 — not the crown.
4. Build broad third-party presence to lift mention frequency; sharpen category-specific proof to lift position.
5. Ship Schema.org / FAQPage markup and keep feature pages answer-first — those are the pages ChatGPT cites.

## How we measure

DecaGEO runs a consistent set of CRM recommendation prompts each week through ChatGPT (GPT-5.4, US region) and records which brands are named and how prominently.

- **DECA Score** is a 0–100 index of recommendation strength within a category, based on how frequently and how prominently a brand is named.
- **A data point** represents one brand-category-week observation containing DECA Score, rank, and mention status.
- **Model labels** (e.g., GPT-5.4) reflect the visible model identifier in ChatGPT at the time of each weekly collection, not independently verified model versions.
- **Limitations:** A single week's change is an observation, not a trend. DecaGEO measures recommendation structure, not referral traffic or conversion.
