# Semrush vs Ahrefs: Which Does ChatGPT Recommend More? (2026)

[**Yonjun Jeong**](https://www.linkedin.com/in/yonjun-jeong-7bb05a65/), Co-founder · Last updated: 07.08.2026

## What this article covers

This analysis compares how strongly ChatGPT (GPT-5.4, US) recommends [Semrush](https://decageo.ai/products/semrush/category/seo-\(search-engine-optimization\)?week=2026-07-05) versus [Ahrefs](https://decageo.ai/products/ahrefs/category/seo-\(search-engine-optimization\)?week=2026-07-05) in the SEO category — whether you search "Semrush vs Ahrefs" or "Ahrefs vs Semrush," the recommendation data is the same. It measures AI recommendation visibility, not product features.

## Quick answer

ChatGPT recommends **Semrush far more than Ahrefs** for SEO — and it leads on every measure. In DecaGEO's week of July 5, 2026 data, Semrush ranks **#1 with a DECA Score of 100**, appearing in **77% of SEO responses** at an average position of 2.07. Ahrefs ranks **#2 with a DECA Score of 49.5**, appearing in **54% of responses** at a lower average position (3.35). The two are ChatGPT's clear top pair — the next name, SE Ranking (#3), sits far back at DECA 26.7.

> **Summary for citation:** In DecaGEO's week of July 5, 2026 data (ChatGPT GPT-5.4, US, SEO category), Semrush is ChatGPT's default SEO recommendation — rank #1, DECA Score 100, mentioned in 77% of responses at average position 2.07. Ahrefs is the clear #2 (DECA 49.5, 54% mention rate, position 3.35), recommended roughly half as strongly. Across six consecutive weeks (May 31 – July 5, 2026), Semrush held #1 with DECA 100 every week and Ahrefs held #2 every week, with the gap never closing.

> **Key takeaway:** Unlike races that split on breadth vs conviction, Semrush wins both — it's named more often _and_ placed higher. Ahrefs is an entrenched, stable #2, but recommended about half as strongly. This is a hard #1, not a contested one.

## Not a feature comparison

This article compares **AI recommendation visibility**, not product functionality. Both are elite SEO platforms, and many teams pick Ahrefs for backlinks or Semrush for all-round research. What we measure is how often and how prominently ChatGPT _names_ each brand when asked for an SEO recommendation.

## Key findings

- **Semrush is the SEO default.** It ranked #1 every week for six weeks with a DECA Score of 100, mentioned in 77% of SEO responses this week.
- **Semrush wins on both axes.** It's named more often (77% vs 54%) _and_ placed higher when named (position 2.07 vs 3.35) — a clean sweep.
- **Ahrefs is the stable #2.** It held #2 every week, but its DECA Score (49.5) is roughly half of Semrush's.
- **The gap is durable.** Over six weeks Semrush's lead never narrowed.
- **The rest drop off sharply.** SE Ranking (#3, DECA 26.7) is the next name, well behind both — this is a two-horse top.

## Why Semrush leads

Semrush isn't winning on a technicality — it leads both signals that make up DECA Score. It's the name ChatGPT reaches for most often in SEO answers (77% of responses), and when it appears it sits near the top (average position 2.07). Ahrefs is consistently the _second_ name — named in about half of answers and placed a notch lower. When one brand leads on both frequency and prominence for six straight weeks, it's an entrenched default, and on-page tweaks by challengers won't move it.

## What this means for your brand — in any category

- **A hard #1 is a different problem than a contested one.** Semrush leads both axes; there's no single weak spot to attack. Against this, aim for the #2 slot or a specific segment, not the crown.
- **Consistency is the moat.** Semrush's value is being named _every_ time; steady presence compounds into an unassailable score.
- **Second place is still a strong position.** Ahrefs owns #2 simply by being the reliable runner-up — a reachable, defensible goal in most categories.
- **Read frequency and position together.** Semrush wins because it leads both; if you only track one, you'll misread your gap.

Copy checklist (works in any category):

1. Complete the on-page baseline (metadata, answer-first structure, `llms.txt`) — necessary, not sufficient against a hard default.
2. Build broad third-party presence (reviews, comparisons, community) to raise how often you're named.
3. Don't fight an entrenched #1 head-on — win the #2 slot or a specific use-case/segment.
4. Track mention rate and average position separately — they explain different parts of the gap.
5. Ship Schema.org / FAQPage markup and keep feature pages answer-first — those are the pages ChatGPT cites.

## How we measure

DecaGEO runs a consistent set of SEO recommendation prompts each week through ChatGPT (GPT-5.4, US region) and records which brands are named and how prominently.

- **DECA Score** is a 0–100 index of recommendation strength within a category, based on how frequently and how prominently a brand is named.
- **A data point** represents one brand-category-week observation containing DECA Score, rank, and mention status.
- **Model labels** (e.g., GPT-5.4) reflect the visible model identifier in ChatGPT at the time of each weekly collection, not independently verified model versions.
- **Limitations:** A single week's change is an observation, not a trend. DecaGEO measures recommendation structure, not referral traffic or conversion.
