# How DecaGEO Works

DecaGEO is the first standardized ranking system for AI brand visibility. We measure which brands AI platforms actually recommend, how often, and how prominently.

## What We Measure

Every week, we send hundreds of recommendation-seeking questions to AI platforms, modeled on how real buyers ask for help. We analyze the responses and produce three core metrics.

**DECA Score (0-100):** A composite score reflecting how frequently and how prominently AI recommends a brand. Higher positions earn more points.

**Mention Rate (%):** The percentage of AI responses that include a given brand as a recommended solution.

**Rank:** The overall chart position within a category, determined by DECA Score.

## How Rankings Are Generated

**1. We design buyer-perspective questions.** Our queries simulate real buyer scenarios, factoring in conditions like team size, budget, use case, and industry. This goes beyond generic prompts like "best CRM software."

**2. We ask AI platforms.** Questions are sent to AI platforms using a fixed model and version. Currently tracking ChatGPT (GPT-5.4) for the United States market in English.

**3. We count recommendations only.** We only count instances where AI recommends a brand as a solution. Passing mentions, negative references, and comparison filler are excluded.

**4. We calculate and publish.** DECA Scores are calculated, brands are ranked, and the results are published as weekly-updated charts on [decageo.ai](https://decageo.ai).

## What Makes This Different

**Persona-driven measurement.** We design queries from the buyer's perspective, not generic keyword prompts. Real decision conditions like team size, budget, and use case produce richer, more actionable rankings.

**Recommendation-only counting.** Not every brand mention is a recommendation. We filter out passing mentions, negative references, and comparison filler to measure actual endorsement.

**Fixed methodology.** One model, one version, one collection window per week. Week-to-week changes in DECA Score reflect real shifts in AI behavior, not measurement noise.

**Segment-level insights.** A brand that ranks #1 overall might drop to #5 for enterprise buyers. Our condition filters reveal these differences.
